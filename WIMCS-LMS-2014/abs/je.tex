{\bf Abstract.}
Solving the equation of motion for a vibrating string with inhomogeneous mass distribution on a semi-axis by means of Fourierâ€™s method leads to a particular Sturm-Liouville spectral problem. The solution of the corresponding inverse spectral problem, describing the totality of all associated Weyl-Titchmarsh functions, goes back to work of M. G. Krein from the 1950s and of L. de Branges from the 1960s. I will review this result and show how it can be generalized to strings with indefinite mass distribution. This talk is based on joint work with A. Kostenko.
